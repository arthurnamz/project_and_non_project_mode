## Motivation letter

1. **Introduction (1 short paragraph)**

   * Briefly introduce yourself (academic background, research field).
   * State *what* you’re applying for (PhD program + university).
   * Include a concise motivation — *why this program or topic interests you*.

2. **Academic background & research experience (1–2 paragraphs)**

   * Highlight your *academic foundation* — key courses, grades, and research projects relevant to the PhD.
   * Describe any *research, internships, or theses*: what you did, what you learned, and the outcome (publications, presentations, etc.).
   * Emphasize *skills* (technical, analytical, or methodological) that prepare you for the PhD.

3. **Research interests and fit (1–2 paragraphs)**

   * Explain your *current research interests* and *how they align* with the group, supervisor, or department.
   * Mention specific professors, labs, or ongoing projects you find inspiring.
   * Show that you’ve *done your homework* — refer to the department’s methods, approaches, or recent work.

4. **Motivation and goals (1 paragraph)**

   * Explain *why you want to do a PhD*: your curiosity, drive for discovery, and long-term career goals.
   * Link how this program helps you achieve those goals (skills, environment, mentorship).

5. **Conclusion (short, confident)**

   * Reiterate your enthusiasm and readiness.
   * End with a positive, polite statement (“I would be honored to contribute to…”, “I look forward to the opportunity to…”).
